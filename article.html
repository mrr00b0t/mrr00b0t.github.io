A Simple Guide to English Articles

Mastering when to use 'a,' 'an,' 'the,' or no article at all.

1

Indefinite: A / AN

Use:

Non-specific, singular nouns. Used when mentioning something for the first time.

Rule (Based on SOUND):

The choice between 'a' and 'an' depends on the initial sound of the next word, not the spelling.

  • A

    before a consonant sound.

    e.g., a book, a house, a university ('y' sound)

  • AN

    before a vowel sound.

    e.g., an apple, an idea, an hour ('h' is silent)

2

Definite: THE

Use:

Specific nouns. Unique items. Things everyone in the conversation already knows.

When to use 'the':

  • Already mentioned: I read a book. The book was good.
  • Unique items: the sun, the moon, the sky
  • Superlatives: the best, the tallest
  • Geography: the Himalayas, the Nile, the USA
  • Places/Things: the cinema, play the guitar

Use:

General concepts, uncountable nouns, and most proper nouns.

When to use no article:

  • General Ideas: love, happiness, education
  • Meals: have breakfast, eat dinner
  • Institutions (purpose): go to school, in hospital
  • Most Places: Japan, Paris, Mount Fuji
  • Activities/Subjects: play football, study history
